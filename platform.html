Governor · Nathan Sanchez Alvarado

Governance, Education & Expansion

Sanchez

Education Infrastructure

MSL's educational value has always been real — but it has been informal and uneven across campuses. The goal is to make it explicit, transferable, and credit-bearing so that every member can walk away with something legible and lasting.

  • Develop and package a standardized credit course curriculum that any member institution can adopt through its political science or social sciences department
  • Build the curriculum around practical legislative skills: bill drafting, floor procedure, committee process, and policy analysis
  • Work with faculty advisors at member institutions to pursue academic credit recognition through each school's registrar
  • Pursue University System of Maryland recognition to make credit transferable across state institutions
Sanchez

Transparency & Institutional Governance

MSL's operations should be legible to its own members. That means regular reporting, clear governance standards, and a leadership structure that distributes responsibility rather than concentrating it in one office.

  • Publish semesterly impact reports covering membership, legislation submitted, events held, and fiscal activity
  • Establish an appointed cabinet mirroring the Maryland Governor's office — Secretaries for Civic Education, Member Development, Digital Infrastructure, External Affairs, and Delegation Development
  • Annual State of MSL address delivered to the Council of State and Board of Directors
  • Leadership succession protocol ensuring incoming officers receive documented briefings and system access within two weeks of election
  • Annual review and public status update on all Project 2030 commitments
Sanchez

Chapter Expansion & Delegation Development

Maryland has more than 30 colleges without active MSL chapters. The infrastructure to activate them exists but what has been missing is a dedicated person and a systematic approach. This plank fixes that.

  • Appoint a Secretary of Delegation Development to research and prioritize viable inactive campuses based on political science programs, student government infrastructure, and geographic distribution
  • Produce a Delegation Starter Kit: founding documents, a recruitment script, a sample first meeting agenda, and regional support contacts
  • Tie the credit course curriculum directly to delegation growth; campuses that adopt the course have a built-in recruitment and retention mechanism
  • Implement annual delegation health metrics tracking membership size, event attendance rate, bills submitted, and leadership continuity
  • Target: 250 students at Annual Session by 2030 - enough to seat both chambers, all committees, and all leadership roles simultaneously
Sanchez

Fundraising & Financial Development

MSL cannot grow its programming, reduce member fees, or invest in new resources without growing its revenue. This plank treats fundraising not as a one-time effort but as an ongoing organizational capability built at both the statewide and chapter level.

  • Pursue at least one external grant application per fiscal year, targeting the Venable Foundation, Abell Foundation, Maryland Humanities Council, and similar Maryland-based funders
  • Develop a statewide events strategy that generates revenue while adding member value such mixers, speaker series, legislative receptions, and cross-campus competitions
  • Appoint a Financial Development Committee to assess how individual chapters currently raise funds, identify best practices, and produce a replicable fundraising playbook that any delegation can implement
  • Recognize that every chapter operates under different university finance rules, the committee's role is to understand those differences and provide tailored guidance, not a one-size-fits-all mandate
  • Direct increased revenue toward reducing event fees, funding delegation travel, and investing in shared digital infrastructure
Sanchez

Civic Engagement & Town Halls

MSL should be visible in the civic life of its member campuses and surrounding communities, not just at annual events. A structured town hall and civic engagement program puts MSL in direct conversation with students, faculty, and community members about the issues that matter to them.

  • Establish a recurring town hall format that delegations can host on their own campuses. Structured public forums where MSL members facilitate discussion on local, state, and national policy issues
  • Train MSL members to serve as civic facilitators, not just legislators, running accessible public conversations that bring non-members into the process
  • Partner with student governments, academic departments, and community organizations to co-host events and expand reach beyond existing MSL membership
  • Use town halls as a recruitment pipeline, students who engage with MSL through a public event are more likely to join a delegation
  • Document and share town hall outcomes as part of the semesterly impact report

Lt. Governor · Timi Daramola

Civic Education, Community & Opportunity

Daramola

Community Engagement & Voter Drives

MSL delegations should be active and visible in their own communities, not just at annual sessions. This means building frameworks that help delegations go beyond campus and make a tangible, measurable impact on civic participation where they live and study.

  • Develop a community engagement toolkit that any delegation can deploy: templates, talking points, and event formats for local civic outreach
  • Launch a coordinated voter registration and voter drive program across all active MSL campuses, timed around election cycles at the local, state, and federal level
  • Train MSL members to run peer-to-peer voter outreach, equipping them with the skills to register fellow students, explain ballot issues, and facilitate informed participation
  • Partner with campus student governments and community organizations to amplify voter drive reach beyond MSL membership
  • Track and report voter registration numbers and drive participation as part of the annual civic impact report; making MSL's contribution to democratic participation visible and measurable
  • Create cross-campus programming that brings delegations together between sessions around shared civic goals
Daramola

Internship & Career Pipeline

MSL members have access to legislative internships, policy fellowships, and civic leadership programs but that access is inconsistent and often depends on who you know. The goal is to systematize it so every member benefits, not just the connected ones.

  • Build and maintain an internship tracker, a regularly updated database of legislative internship and policy fellowship opportunities refreshed every semester by a designated cabinet appointee
  • Develop resume and interview preparation resources tailored specifically to legislative and policy roles
  • Connect MSL alumni with current members for mentorship, referrals, and career guidance
  • Track and report placement outcomes annually so the pipeline's impact is documented and the program improves over time
  • Coordinate with the Secretary of External Affairs to build relationships with legislative offices, advocacy organizations, and policy institutions that recruit MSL members directly
Daramola

Expanding Civic Education Resources

MSL's civic education resources are already strong. The challenge is distribution, accessibility, and format. Timi's focus is ensuring those resources reach every member at every school and adding new formats that meet students where they are.

  • Centralize and expand MSL's civic education library from bill drafting guides, Parli Pro references, legislative research tools and make it accessible to all member institutions
  • Produce a short-form video series explaining key MSL and civic concepts: how to draft a bill, how floor debate works, what parliamentary procedure looks like in practice, how to read legislation, and more
  • Design videos to be shareable and platform-agnostic, usable in delegation meetings, LTI sessions, classrooms, and on social media for broader civic outreach
  • Ensure all deliverables are formatted for use at any campus, but keep each chapters unique flair.
  • Coordinate with the Secretary of Civic Education on statewide rollout and adoption tracking
Daramola

Recruitment Infrastructure

Growing MSL requires more than enthusiasm. It requires systems and tools that delegations can use to recruit consistently, onboard new members effectively, and retain people across more than one semester.

  • Build and maintain a recruitment resource hub for delegation leaders: templates, outreach scripts, social media toolkits, and event planning guides
  • Develop onboarding frameworks so new members understand MSL's structure, purpose, and expectations from day one
  • Create incentive structures that reward consistent participation: recognition, leadership pathways, and tangible benefits tied to engagement
  • Support inactive chapter reactivation with targeted outreach, starter resources, and direct contact from the Lt. Governor's office
  • Work alongside the Secretary of Delegation Development to ensure recruitment efforts align with the broader chapter expansion strategy
Strategic Framework

These platform commitments are structured within a five-year institutional plan. Project 2030 provides the milestones, timelines, and annual review requirements so this work continues beyond any single term.
