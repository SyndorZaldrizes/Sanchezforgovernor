Platform & Policy Priorities

Home Platform Contact

This platform is built on the principle that student governance should be measured by the clarity of its commitments and the precision of its execution. The Maryland Student Legislature does not need empty rhetoric. It needs operational improvements tied to specific governance mechanisms and documented outcomes.

Each plank below outlines the context of our current success, our targeted policy goals, a structured implementation pathway, and measurable outcomes for the organization.

1. Education Enhancements

Context and Opportunity

The Maryland Student Legislature has long been a premier venue for civic education. We have an opportunity to build on this foundation by formalizing our educational programming to ensure every delegate has the tools to succeed in legislative drafting and substantive policy debate.

Policy Goals

  • Standardize legislative drafting workshops across all active chapters to improve the technical quality of submitted bills.
  • Establish formal academic partnerships with Maryland university departments to align MSL participation with recognized learning outcomes.
  • Expand digital resources to include structured tutorials on parliamentary procedure and fiscal analysis.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Assemble an Education Working Group to develop a standardized "Legislative Toolkit" for chapter use.

Phase 2: Roll out the toolkit to chapter presidents and faculty advisors during the fall leadership retreat.

Governance: Update the executive programming mandate through a board resolution to include semesterly training benchmarks.

Measurable Outcomes

Target a 30% reduction in technical bill drafting errors and a 15% increase in cross-disciplinary membership through academic department outreach.

2. Transparency & Integrity

Context and Opportunity

Organizational integrity is the bedrock of our legitimacy. MSL already maintains high standards; our goal is to evolve these into a modernized, transparent reporting framework that makes our executive operations and chapter successes visible to every member.

Policy Goals

  • Institutionalize the Semesterly MSL Impact Report to document organizational progress and executive decisions.
  • Standardize governance practices across chapters through a digital reporting dashboard.
  • Enhance executive-to-chapter communication through monthly "Town Hall" updates on board initiatives.

Implementation Plan

Short-term: Establish a standardized reporting template for all executive board members.

Medium-term: Implement a public-facing "Transparency Portal" on the MSL internal site for archived meeting minutes and reports.

Governance: Propose a bylaws amendment to codify the publication of Impact Reports as a permanent organizational requirement.

Measurable Outcomes

100% compliance with semesterly reporting deadlines and a documented increase in member engagement with executive board proceedings.

3. Chapter Expansion & Support

Context and Opportunity

Growth is a sign of a healthy institution. By refining our support systems for existing chapters and creating clear pathways for new activations, we can ensure that MSL remains the leading voice for student advocacy in Maryland.

Policy Goals

  • Increase active MSL membership by 25% through targeted recruitment and chapter reactivation strategies.
  • Develop a comprehensive "Officer Training Framework" to ensure leadership continuity within chapters.
  • Implement a proactive "Retention System" to identify and support struggling chapters before they become inactive.

Implementation Plan

Strategic Start: Conduct a state-wide "Chapter Health Audit" to identify specific resource needs at each institution.

Operational: Deploy Executive Board liaisons to assist in "Activation Workshops" at targeted colleges and universities.

Training: Establish a mentorship program pairing experienced chapter presidents with newly elected officers.

Measurable Outcomes

Activation of at least two new or dormant chapters and a 25% net increase in statewide delegate registration.

4. Fiscal Responsibility

Context and Opportunity

Responsible stewardship of our resources allows us to fund the programming that matters most. We will build on current budgeting practices by introducing more granular reporting and exploring new avenues for revenue growth through strategic partnerships.

Policy Goals

  • Implement disciplined budget analysis for all MSL programming to maximize cost-effectiveness.
  • Diversify revenue streams through a formal sponsorship and fundraising strategy.
  • Provide chapters with standardized financial reporting tools to ensure fiscal alignment across the organization.

Implementation Plan

Immediate: Conduct a comprehensive review of all recurring organizational expenses to identify optimization opportunities.

Growth: Establish a "Development Subcommittee" tasked with securing institutional and community sponsorships.

Reporting: Transition to a digital ledger system that allows for real-time tracking of MSL budget status.

Measurable Outcomes

A 15% increase in non-dues revenue and a balanced budget with a documented reduction in administrative overhead.

5. Civic Engagement

Context and Opportunity

MSL is a catalyst for engaged democracy. We will strengthen the quality of our debate and our public outreach, ensuring that our legislative sessions serve as a high-impact platform for student-led civic discourse.

Policy Goals

  • Enhance the technical quality of legislative debate through structured parliamentary training and guest speaker series.
  • Expand public outreach by formalizing MSL’s role in Maryland-wide civic literacy initiatives.
  • Create structured engagement opportunities for members to interact with state policymakers and advocacy groups.

Implementation Plan

Programming: Integrate "Debate Mastery" modules into regular MSL sessions and chapter meetings.

Partnership: Coordinate with Maryland civic organizations to host joint forums on legislative priorities.

Advocacy: Establish an "External Relations" framework to bridge the gap between MSL resolutions and real-world policy discussions.

Measurable Outcomes

A documented improvement in debate quality metrics and a 20% increase in attendance at MSL-hosted public forums.

6. High School Outreach Initiative

Context and Opportunity

Preparing the next generation of MSL delegates begins in high school. We will establish a pilot outreach program that introduces Maryland high school students to the mechanics of student legislature, cultivating early civic literacy and a pipeline for future leadership.

Policy Goals

  • Design and launch a pilot High School Outreach program integrated within the existing MSL chapter structure.
  • Define clear oversight responsibilities within the Executive Board for high school mentorship.
  • Develop a simplified "Civic Literacy Curriculum" tailored for high school model legislation workshops.

Implementation Plan

Pilot Phase: Launch outreach partnerships with three Maryland high schools, led by nearby MSL college chapters.

Resource Allocation: Create a dedicated "Outreach Toolkit" containing simplified legislative templates and procedure guides.

Evaluation: Monitor student participation and knowledge retention through post-workshop assessments.

Measurable Outcomes

Participation from at least 150 high school students in the pilot year and a 10% conversion rate of outreach participants into MSL college delegates.
