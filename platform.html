This platform is built around measurable growth, structural clarity, and institutional accountability. The goal is not symbolic change, but operational improvement across the Maryland Student Legislature.

1. Chapter Expansion & Support

Strategic Growth Model

Increase active statewide membership by 25% within one academic year through targeted chapter mentorship, leadership training, and structured onboarding processes.

Establish a formal support network where experienced chapters provide operational guidance to new or restarting chapters, ensuring sustainability beyond one semester cycles.

2. Chapters as Civic Hubs

Institutional Engagement Framework

Transform chapters into structured civic centers that host quarterly town halls, policy forums, and legislative awareness events.

Integrate virtual participation tools to expand accessibility and allow cross-campus collaboration on complex policy discussions.

3. High School Outreach Pilot

Pipeline Development

Launch a pilot program partnering established chapters with local high schools to introduce mini-model legislatures and civic literacy workshops.

Develop scalable documentation so the program can expand statewide if successful.

4. Performance & Accountability

Metrics & Transparency

Implement measurable engagement tracking, including membership growth, event participation, and intercollegiate session involvement.

Publish a semesterly “MSL Impact Report” to provide transparent data on organizational performance and executive initiatives.
