The Maryland Student Legislature works best when its leadership understands how institutions actually function — and is willing to do the work to improve them. That is what this campaign is about.

About Nathan

I serve as DCP of the Maryland Student Legislature, working at the intersection of policy design, governance structure, and civic education. My approach to leadership is grounded in process, and action. I’ve worked directly with Maryland state delegates and senators, studied how committees operate, and spent time understanding how legislative language becomes binding policy.

Through my work in MSL and alongside Maryland state legislators, I’ve studied how policy moves from idea to enforceable language. That experience shapes how I approach student governance: with attention to drafting precision, fiscal impact, constitutional alignment, and realistic implementation.

In MSL, I care about building an environment where delegates understand not just what they are voting on, but how the process itself works. When delegates understand committee mechanics, parliamentary procedure, and the logic behind amendments, debate becomes stronger and outcomes improve.

Outside of formal sessions, I’m someone who thinks in systems: political, economic, technological. That curiosity is what brought me to MSL in the first place. Institutions matter. Structure matters. And when they’re built correctly, they amplify the people inside them. Because YOU are the future.

Platform Vision

Six priority areas, each tied to specific governance mechanisms and measurable outcomes:

Education Enhancements

Strengthen MSL's educational programming, align chapter activities with academic support goals, and enhance academic partnerships with organization

Transparency & Integrity

Publish semesterly impact reports. Enforce clear governance standards across chapters. Make MSL's operations legible to its own members.

Chapter Expansion & Support

Grow active MSL Membership by 25% in the next academic year including supporting resources for non active chapters.

Fiscal Responsibility

Apply disciplined budget analysis to MSL's resource allocation. Ensure funds are directed toward programs with documented impact and increase revenue streams.

Civic Engagement

Expanding constitutional and legislative education through MSL chapters and using MSL as a catalyst for engaged democracy .

High School Outreach Initiative

Establishing a pilot program to align MSL structure and programming into High Schools, cultivating early civic literacy
